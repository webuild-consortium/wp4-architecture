## Terms and Definitions

**Access Certificate**: A certificate identifying a party (RP or RPI) as authorized to request 
attribute presentations from EUDIWs.

**Embedded Disclosure Policy**: A policy included within an Electronic Attestation of Attributes 
(EAA) that specifies which types of Relying Parties are authorized to receive the attributes, 
as defined in Annex III of CIR 2024/2979.

**Dual Identification**: The requirement for wallets to correctly identify and display both the 
Relying Party Intermediary and the actual Relying Party in intermediary-mediated transactions.

**Registration Certificate**: A certificate issued by a member state that specifies which attributes 
the RP is authorized to request and, if a Relying Party Intermediary is involved, links the Relying 
Party and Relying Party Intermediary.

**Online Registrar**: A service operated by a member state that provides real-time verification 
of RP registration status and RP-RPI relationships, used when Registration Certificates 
are not available.

**Relying Party Intermediary (RPI)**: A party that provides a service to Relying Parties related 
to the handling of the interaction with wallets for identification, 
authentication or attribute attestation presentation. Also called "identity broker" in some contexts. 
Recognized in eIDAS 2 Article 5b(10).
